# Premium Typography And Layout Rules

Premium UI depends on disciplined type, spacing, and composition more than
decoration.

## Font Pairing

Use separate display and body fonts where possible:

- Instrument Serif italic + Inter for luxury editorial contrast.
- Instrument Serif italic + Barlow for expressive portfolio/agency pages.
- Readex Pro + Readex Pro for soft contemporary SaaS.
- Inter + Inter for neutral product UI.
- Geist + Inter for black-white developer SaaS.

## Hero Typography

- Very large size.
- Line-height `0.8` to `0.95`.
- Letter-spacing `-0.02em` to `-0.05em`.
- Font-weight `400` to `500`.
- Short memorable lines.
- Avoid default `font-bold` unless brand voice requires it.

## Section Typography

- Section headings: `text-4xl` to `text-6xl`.
- Tight line-height.
- Body copy: small but readable.
- Dark body copy: `text-white/60` to `text-white/80`.
- Max paragraph width: `32ch` to `64ch`.
- Do not stretch paragraphs too wide.

## Composition

Use asymmetry and whitespace. On desktop, alternate dense information and open
space. On landing pages, avoid making every section centered. Use large media
fields, editorial margins, and scale contrast.

Useful layouts:

- Centered poster hero.
- Giant staggered type.
- Split text/media only when the media is immersive and not a generic card.
- 7/5 and 5/7 grids.
- Magazine-like feature rows.
- Bento grids with varied card sizes.

## Negative Rules

Avoid same-size card grids everywhere, random gradients, weak text contrast,
crowded navs, body copy wider than the reader can scan, and default SaaS
typography with `font-bold` hero headings.
