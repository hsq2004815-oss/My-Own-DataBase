# Premium Typography And Layout Rules

Premium UI depends on disciplined type, spacing, and composition more than
decoration.

## Font Pairing

Use separate display and body fonts where possible:

- Instrument Serif italic + Inter for luxury editorial contrast.
- Instrument Serif italic + Barlow for expressive portfolio/agency pages.
- Readex Pro + Readex Pro for soft contemporary SaaS.
- Inter + Inter for neutral product UI.
- Geist + Inter for black-white developer SaaS.

## Hero Typography

- Very large size.
- Line-height `0.8` to `0.95`.
- Letter-spacing `-0.02em` to `-0.05em`.
- Font-weight `400` to `500`.
- Short memorable lines.
- Avoid default `font-bold` unless brand voice requires it.

## Section Typography

- Section headings: `text-4xl` to `text-6xl`.
- Tight line-height.
- Body copy: small but readable.
- Dark body copy: `text-white/60` to `text-white/80`.
- Max paragraph width: `32ch` to `64ch`.
- Do not stretch paragraphs too wide.

## Composition

Use asymmetry and whitespace. On desktop, alternate dense information and open
space. On landing pages, avoid making every section centered. Use large media
fields, editorial margins, and scale contrast.

Useful layouts:

- Centered poster hero.
- Top-aligned cinematic campaign hero with uppercase giant type.
- Bottom-left cinematic poster hero with directional gradients.
- Centered SaaS / app video hero with tagline pill and dual CTA.
- Two-panel Liquid Glass split hero with left narrative and right information
  cluster.
- Technical education HLS hero with vertical grid, SVG glow, floating glass
  info card, and single accent punctuation.
- Giant staggered type.
- Split text/media only when the media is immersive and not a generic card.
- 7/5 and 5/7 grids.
- Magazine-like feature rows.
- Bento grids with varied card sizes.

## Hero Font Role Splits

High-end hero pages can use multiple fonts only when each has a clear role.

- App / SaaS video hero: Manrope for UI/navigation, Cabin for buttons/tags,
  Instrument Serif for headline, Inter for body.
- Strict Liquid Glass brand hero: Poppins for display/body, Source Serif 4 for
  serif accent.
- Technical education hero: Inter Extra Bold for headline, Plus Jakarta Sans
  for eyebrow, Instrument Serif italic for one emphasis word.

Serif italic works best for short emphasis inside headings. Do not use many
fonts randomly, and do not turn full body copy into decorative serif.

## Single Accent Punctuation

A premium title does not always need a full gradient. A restrained accent on one
word, punctuation mark, slash, bracket, cursor, or final period is often more
premium, especially for technical and grayscale hero pages.

## Negative Rules

Avoid same-size card grids everywhere, random gradients, weak text contrast,
crowded navs, body copy wider than the reader can scan, and default SaaS
typography with `font-bold` hero headings. Avoid full-line gradient headlines
when a single accent punctuation would create stronger hierarchy.
